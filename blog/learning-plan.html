My Learning Plan:

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

My Long term to goal is to build a career as software engineer, learn to invest wisely so I can retire as early as possible, and one day use my skills to help those in need. I don’t know what that looks like exactly but I do know that software engineering and web-development are areas I believe I can be good at and enjoy!

Describe your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning

Strengths:

  • Very motivated, once I decide I on what it is I want to do I am very motivated to do it.
  • I like to do things in order and not skip important parts. I understand the importance of getting the basics right before moving on to more complicated things.
  • I learn best from repetition and practice.
  • I am very committed! Once I decide to do something I will give it my all.
  • Not afraid to try to learn new things!
  • Plan out my day so that I am organized and have direction.

Limitations:

  • Can get distracted sometimes, either from people, devices, or floating thoughts.
  • Taking to long to complete tasks. Slow paced learning and/or distractions.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

  1. Better learning capability and developed learning strageties that work best for me.
  2. Better time management skills.
  3. Become a faster learner.
  4. Better problem solving skills.

How will you comit to managing yourself to work productively and safely?

  • I have a planned structure that I have printed out next to me to keep myself on task and organised each day. To be productive I will focus mainly on three things, 1. First have a plan prepared at the beginning of the day or prepared the night before on how I intend to execute my day. The plan is clear and has time slots allocated to specifically chosen tasks that are to be completed that day. I know that if I don’t have this structure in place and try to wing it, I get less done and can easily loose sight on is important to focus on and how much time I am spending on it if it is too much or not enough etc.
  • Discipline and Focus are important to maintain for me as well. For me, understanding and having a WHY is what will keep me on task. If I get distracted or something grabs my attention I need to ask myself is important or do I need to focus and get back on track? Ask myself why am I here? Are my goals more important then that distraction? ABSOLUTELY!!!!
  • If things get tough and they start to build up, I have my facilitator to talk to, also have other support as well. If I am stuck on something or just need to get my mind of things to refresh, I like to go out to nature for walks and breathe some fresh air, do some exercise and then come back to the problem again with fresh eyes.

How will you seek help in a timely manner?

So far, I have been leasing with my course Facilitator and she has been great with any issues so far. This works for me so will stick to this approach unless I need to do something different.

What are my expectations for the Facilitation team?

I think it is important for them to provide the guidance and support that is needed to help students get through the course. And also provide a push every now and then if they can see we have more to give.
